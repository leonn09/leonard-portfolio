Dennis Ritchie

The man who shaped modern computing

Dennis Ritchie stands over Ken Thompson as he works on the PDP-11 in 1972.

Here's a time line of Dennis Ritchie's life:

  • 1941 - Born in Bronxville, New York, USA
  • 1963 - Earns a bachelor's degree in physics from Harvard University
  • 1968 - Completes a Ph.D. in mathematics from Harvard
  • 1967 - Joins Bell Labs, where he begins working on the Multics
  • 1969 - Teams up with Ken Thompson to create Unix
  • 1972 - Creates the C programming language and rewrites Unix in it
  • 1978 - Publishes The C Programming Language book
  • 1983 - Wins the Turing Award with Ken Thompson
  • 1990s - Works on Plan 9 and Inferno (Unix evolution)
  • 1999 - Wins National Medal of Technology with Thompson
  • 2007 - Retires from Lucent Technologies
  • 2011 - Passes away at age 70
